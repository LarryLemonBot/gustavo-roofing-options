What to include

Send the details that help Vera's Roofing understand the property faster.

The first conversation is easier when the message includes the town, property type, roof concern, a few photos if available, and any access notes.

Homeowners

Send photos of the area you are worried about, the room or exterior side affected, and whether you are seeing a leak, missing shingles, storm damage, or an aging roof surface.

Rental properties

Include whether the property is occupied, vacant, or between tenants, plus the best access notes and any photos from the tenant, owner, or property manager.

Business properties

Share the roof area, access constraints, low-slope or metal details, and any timing concerns around customers, staff, tenants, or normal business operations.

Before you reach out

Send the details that make the first roofing conversation useful.

What should I include when I text Vera's Roofing?

Include the town, property type, what you are seeing, and a few clear roof photos if available. Mention whether the issue is a leak, storm damage, aging shingles, metal detail, low-slope area, or replacement planning.

Should I call or text first?

Text photos when the roof concern is easier to show. Call when the issue needs a direct conversation. Email works well when you are collecting details for a planned home, rental, or business-property project.

Can photos replace an inspection?

Photos help start a practical roofing conversation, but they do not replace every inspection need. Vera's Roofing can use the photos to understand the concern and talk through the next practical step.
