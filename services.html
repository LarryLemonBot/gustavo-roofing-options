Hand-nailed shingle work

Shingle fastening is one place where precision still matters.

When hand-nailing is part of your replacement scope, the crew can check nail placement and depth before the next shingle covers it. That matters on coastal homes where wind exposure punishes weak fastening.

Good fit for: Homeowners comparing shingle replacement options, coastal properties exposed to wind, and owners who want to know what to ask about fastening care, nail placement, and nail depth before choosing a shingle replacement plan.

Why it matters: Shingles are designed to be fastened in the correct nailing area and held by nails driven flush, not blown through the shingle. Overdriven, crooked, or misplaced nails can cut into the shingle, reduce anchorage, and make wind-related shingle loss more likely.

What to ask: Ask how the crew checks nail placement, nail depth, and corrections when a fastener misses the right spot. Vera's Roofing can explain when hand-nailing makes sense for your roof plan.

Flat-roof waterproofing

EPDM rubber membrane for flat and low-slope roof areas.

EPDM stands for ethylene propylene diene monomer. In plain English, it is a rubber roofing membrane used where shingles are not the right fit, like flat roofs, low-slope sections, and waterproof deck areas.

Good fit for: Flat or low-slope roof sections, covered decks, porch roofs, and small business roof areas where water needs membrane-style waterproofing instead of normal shingles.

Why it matters: The right membrane detail helps protect rooms, decks, and covered areas below from water intrusion in places where normal roof shingles would not shed water correctly.

When to call: Flat roof leaks, low-slope deck areas, membrane concerns, wall-edge transitions, or waterproofing details where ponding water or slow drainage may be part of the concern.

Common roofing questions

Clear answers before you call.

These answers help homeowners, rental owners, and business property owners know what to send and what to expect before the first roofing conversation.

What should I include when I request an inspection?

Send the town, property type, and roof or gutter concern. Photos are optional but helpful when they show the issue.

Can photos help decide between repair and replacement?

Photos can help Vera's Roofing understand what to discuss first, but the right repair-or-replace decision depends on roof age, roof condition, slope, access, and inspection details.

Does every Vera's Roofing job include hand-nailing?

No. Hand-nailing is only part of the work when it is included in the agreed scope. When hand-nailing is part of the scope, each fastener can be placed deliberately and checked for placement and depth.

Can every roof receive CertainTeed manufacturer warranty coverage?

No. CertainTeed warranty availability depends on the selected materials, full roof system, installation requirements, registration, property type, transfer rules, and CertainTeed's written terms. Vera's Roofing can talk through current manufacturer warranty options before materials are ordered.

Do you work on rental and business properties?

Yes. Vera's Roofing works with homeowners, rental property owners, and business property owners across Brunswick County, Wilmington, New Hanover County, and nearby coastal North Carolina.

Do you handle metal, copper, and EPDM roof details?

Vera's Roofing can talk through metal rooflines, copper flashing, roof-wall transitions, EPDM rubber membrane, flat-roof areas, low-slope sections, and other specialty roof details.

Do you offer gutter cleaning and gutter guard installation?

Yes. Vera's Roofing handles gutter cleaning and offers gutter guard installation for homes, rentals, and business properties where overflow, downspout concerns, roof-edge water control, or leaf buildup are part of the concern.
